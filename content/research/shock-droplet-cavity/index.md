## Overview

Conventional shock-droplet studies have treated droplets as homogeneous spheres. Real propellants and atomized fuels, however, frequently contain entrained gas pockets. This project asks a question that — to our knowledge — has not been addressed numerically: **how does an internal cavity change the deformation, baroclinic vorticity generation, and fragmentation of a droplet under a passing shock?**

## What I did

- **Literature review** of shock-droplet and shock-bubble interactions to identify the research gap around cavity-embedded droplets in supersonic flows.
- **Built and validated a numerical framework in STAR-CCM+**, benchmarking against five experimental datasets, analytical solutions, and prior computational studies.
- **Ran shock-droplet simulations** with varying cavity geometries and analyzed Schlieren-style visualizations, baroclinic torque distributions, and pressure field evolution in Python.
